Expectation setting

Trust is built through realistic language

A consultation can explain what may be possible, what may take time, what depends on aftercare, and what cannot be responsibly promised.

  1. Baseline matters. Skin condition, history, routine, and indication shape what improvement can mean.
  2. Response varies. Individual biology, consistency, and aftercare influence progress.
  3. Timing matters. Some goals need staged care, maintenance, or a longer view.

Responsible proof

Good results have a story behind them

The site does not use unsupported claims, invented before-and-afters, or universal promises. Results conversations belong inside assessment, where context can be named clearly.

Proof standard

What responsible proof should avoid

Result content should not imply identical outcomes, hide the role of maintenance, or make online diagnosis sound simple. If patient feedback is added in the future, it should be approved, specific, and framed around experience as much as appearance.
