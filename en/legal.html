This document brings together appointment conditions, consent, authorisations and privacy information related to Franciele Sofiati aesthetic care.

It should be read carefully. The registration form records acknowledgement and acceptance of the points that apply to the selected procedure.

1. Responsible professional identification

This document governs, on a preliminary and complementary basis, registration, appointment booking, consent, authorisations and conditions applicable to aesthetic procedures performed by Franciele Sofiati, Biomedical professional, Esthetician and Cosmetologist, CRBM 6277.

Care takes place at Metropolitan Med Center, Rua Mato Grosso, 1114, Centro, Londrina - PR, 86010-180. The official contact channel is 43 99104-3536 and sofiatimendonca@gmail.com.

2. Nature of the form and assessment

Completing the form does not replace in-person assessment, anamnesis, clinical records, physical examination, skin analysis, contraindication review or individual guidance.

Any procedure depends on professional assessment, schedule availability, confirmation of agreed commercial conditions, safety on the day of care and absence of contraindications identified before or during the appointment.

The professional may confirm, adapt, postpone, interrupt or contraindicate a procedure for technical, ethical, sanitary or safety reasons.

3. Client identification and responsibility for information

The client must provide true and complete data, including name, identification document, CPF when requested, phone or WhatsApp, date of birth, address and email.

If the client is under 18 or requires a legal guardian, the procedure may only continue with guardian authorisation, document submission and prior professional assessment.

Omitting relevant information may increase risks and compromise care safety.

4. Booking, reservation fee and appointment confirmation

A booking fee corresponding to 30% of the total procedure value may be charged to reserve time, room, technical schedule, materials and, when applicable, equipment or technology.

The booking fee is not an additional amount when the client attends and the procedure is performed, because it is deducted from the total on the day of care.

The appointment is only reserved after payment confirmation and submission of the information requested by the clinic.

5. Cancellation, rescheduling and no-show

Rescheduling requested with at least 24 hours notice may keep the fee as credit for a new date, subject to availability.

Cancellation with at least 24 hours notice may generate credit or refund depending on the case, costs already incurred, procedure, reserved equipment and payment method.

Late cancellation, late rescheduling, significant delay or no-show may result in full or partial retention of the booking fee.

6. Professional cancellation and withdrawal rights

The professional may reschedule or cancel due to technical reasons, equipment unavailability, complication, contraindication, safety condition, fortuitous event or force majeure.

For contracts made outside the establishment, statutory withdrawal rights may be exercised within the legal period under applicable law, without removing consumer rights.

7. Procedures covered by this document

This document covers selected procedures including Laser Harmony, LightSheer Duet, botulinum toxin, CO2 AcuPulse laser, Ultraformer MPT, radiofrequency, plasma jet, hair MMP, body or facial MMP, PEIM, mechanical peeling and chemical peeling.

Each procedure has its own purpose, possible benefits, limitations, risks, care instructions, contraindications and specific term to be read and accepted.

8. General risks and no result guarantee

Aesthetic procedures may involve discomfort, pain, redness, swelling, bruising, sensitivity, pigmentation changes, blisters, crusts, infection, scarring, asymmetry, partial result, absence of expected result or need for additional sessions.

No result, duration, degree of improvement, perfect symmetry, complete correction, surgical effect or response identical to another person is promised or guaranteed.

9. Pre- and post-procedure care

The client must follow pre- and post-procedure guidance, including avoiding sun exposure, tanning, excessive heat, manipulation, irritating products, aggressive procedures and unauthorised substances when applicable.

Failure to follow guidance may increase the risk of marks, burns, irritation, infection, scarring, delayed recovery or unsatisfactory results.

10. Photography records and image use

Photographic or audiovisual records of the treated area may be requested for technical monitoring, progress comparison, care documentation, procedure safety and rights defence.

Use of images, videos, before-and-after material or testimonials for marketing is optional and only occurs with specific client authorisation.

11. Privacy, LGPD and digital communication

Personal data and, where applicable, sensitive data are processed for legitimate purposes connected with identification, booking, care, guidance, billing, consent records, legal obligations and rights defence.

The professional or clinic does not sell personal data. Access is limited to the responsible professional and people or providers necessary to the operation.

12. Complications, complaints and dispute resolution

Questions, unexpected reactions, intense discomfort, progressive worsening, signs of infection, burns, important pain, wounds, secretion, fever, allergic reaction or any complication must be reported immediately through the official channel.

The professional may request photos, symptom reports, in-person return, reassessment or referral for medical or urgent care depending on severity.

The client should not self-medicate, manipulate the area, remove crusts, use unauthorised products or follow third-party advice without notifying the responsible professional.
