Registration, consent and safety

Frequently asked questions about the form, booking and consent

These answers follow Franciele Sofiati’s registration, appointment, consent, LGPD privacy and aesthetic procedure authorisation document.

01. What is the registration and appointment form for?

It records identification, appointment rules, general consent, procedure-specific terms, LGPD privacy, image authorisation and complication contact channels before treatment.

02. Does the form replace an in-person assessment?

No. It is preliminary and complementary, and does not replace in-person assessment, anamnesis, clinical records, physical examination, skin analysis or personalised guidance.

03. Who is responsible for the appointment?

Franciele Sofiati, Biomedical professional, Esthetician and Cosmetologist, CRBM 6277.

04. Where does care take place?

At Metropolitan Med Center, Rua Mato Grosso, 1114, Centro, Londrina - PR, 86010-180.

05. Which identification details are requested?

Full name, preferred name, CPF, identity document, phone or WhatsApp, date of birth, address with postcode and email.

06. Can clients under 18 have a procedure?

Only with legal guardian authorisation, the guardian document and prior assessment by the responsible professional.

07. Why is an identity document requested?

To confirm identity, prevent fraud, record consent, organise care and comply with legal or professional obligations.

08. Is the payment receipt required?

Yes. The form requests proof of the appointment fee when the booking has been arranged.

09. What is the 30% appointment fee?

It is a booking fee used to reserve the time, room, materials and, when applicable, the equipment, tip, machine or technology required.

10. Is the appointment fee an extra charge?

No. If the client attends and the procedure is performed, the fee is deducted from the total amount.

11. When is the appointment considered reserved?

Only after payment confirmation of the booking fee and submission of the information requested by the clinic.

12. Can I reschedule with at least 24 hours notice?

Yes. The fee may remain as credit and move to a new date, depending on schedule availability.

13. How does cancellation with 24 hours notice work?

The fee may be converted into credit for another procedure or refunded, depending on the case, costs already incurred and payment method.

14. What happens with less than 24 hours notice?

The clinic may retain the fee to compensate schedule organisation, room reservation, appointment preparation, materials or equipment rental.

15. What happens with significant lateness or no-show?

If the delay prevents safe care or there is no-show without notice, the booking fee may be fully retained.

16. What if equipment was rented for the procedure?

If equipment was booked or rented, late cancellation, late rescheduling or no-show may use the fee to cover that cost in whole or in part.

17. Can the professional cancel or reschedule?

Yes. This may happen for technical reasons, equipment unavailability, complications, contraindications, safety, fortuitous event or force majeure.

18. Is there a right of withdrawal?

For contracts made outside the establishment, the legal right may be exercised within seven calendar days, according to applicable law.

19. Is the procedure guaranteed after filling the form?

No. It depends on assessment, availability, fee confirmation, safety on the day and absence of contraindications.

20. Can the professional adapt or contraindicate the procedure?

Yes. The procedure may be confirmed, adapted, postponed, interrupted or contraindicated for technical, ethical, sanitary or safety reasons.

21. Do I need to choose a procedure on the form?

Yes. The form lists the arranged options, but the final indication depends on professional assessment.

22. Does each procedure have a specific term?

Yes. Laser Harmony, LightSheer, botulinum toxin, CO2, Ultraformer, radiofrequency, plasma jet, MMP, PEIM and peelings have their own terms.

23. Why do I need to accept the specific term?

Because each technology or technique has its own purpose, possible benefits, limitations, risks, aftercare and contraindications.

24. Are aesthetic results guaranteed?

No. The document states there is no promise or guarantee of result, duration, degree of improvement or specific aesthetic response.

25. Why do results vary?

They depend on skin, age, habits, sun exposure, medication, hormones, healing, hydration, diet, individual response and aftercare.

26. Which general risks are disclosed?

Discomfort, pain, stinging, redness, swelling, bruising, sensitivity, pigmentation changes, blisters, crusts, infection, scarring, asymmetry or partial result.

27. What should I disclose before a procedure?

Pregnancy, breastfeeding, allergies, medication, illnesses, infections, wounds, irritation, recent sun, tanning, recent procedures, acids and sensitivities.

28. Can omitting information affect care?

Yes. The document states that omitting relevant information can increase risks, harm results and compromise safety.

29. Should pregnancy or breastfeeding be disclosed?

Yes. Pregnancy, possible pregnancy, trying to conceive and breastfeeding must be disclosed before any procedure.

30. Should allergies and medication be disclosed?

Yes. Allergies, medication, anticoagulants, corticosteroids, isotretinoin, immunosuppressants and other relevant details must be disclosed.

31. Can sun exposure affect treatment?

Yes. Intense sun, tanning and irritated or sensitised skin may increase risks and lead to postponement.

32. Which aftercare is included in the document?

Follow guidance, avoid sun, excessive heat, manipulation and unauthorised products, and report intense pain, discharge, fever, burns or unexpected reactions.

33. When should I contact the clinic after a procedure?

Immediately in case of questions, unexpected reaction, intense discomfort, progressive worsening, signs of infection, burn, significant pain, wound or fever.

34. Can I self-medicate if I have a reaction?

No. The document advises not to self-medicate, manipulate the area or follow third-party advice without contacting the professional.

35. Can the professional request photos during a complication?

Yes. She may request photos, symptom details, in-person review, reassessment or medical referral depending on severity.

36. Does the form authorise technical photos?

It asks whether the client authorises treated-area images for monitoring, comparison, documentation, safety and technical records.

37. Do I have to authorise images for marketing?

No. Use for marketing, social media, the website, ads, portfolio or testimonials is optional and requires specific authorisation.

38. Can I revoke marketing image authorisation?

Yes. Authorisation for new publications can be revoked in writing through the official channel, subject to technical limits and third-party content.

39. Which data does the privacy policy cover?

Name, CPF, document, phone, email, birth date, city, declared information, desired procedure, receipts, photos, before/after images and messages.

40. For which purposes is data processed?

Identification, age confirmation, scheduling, communication, guidance, receipts, billing, consent records, legal obligations, rights defence and digital communication.

41. Can sensitive data be processed?

Yes, when related to the procedure, treated-area images and health information voluntarily provided for safety and follow-up.

42. Where can data be stored?

In digital systems, Formspree, email, WhatsApp, scheduling tools, financial systems, payment platforms, administrative files or secure media adopted by the clinic.

43. Does the clinic sell personal data?

No. The policy states that the professional or clinic does not sell personal data.

44. Who may data be shared with?

People and providers needed for operation, such as form, storage, payment, accounting, legal support and authorities when required by law.

45. What rights do I have over my data?

You may request access, correction, clarification, deletion where applicable, consent withdrawal and information about sharing.

46. Are promotional communications required?

No. The client may authorise news and educational content or accept only communications required for care.

47. Does the form replace a clinical chart?

No. It does not replace clinical records, anamnesis, a chart, physical examination or individual guidance.

48. Why are there terms about lasers and devices?

Because lasers, light, heat, ultrasound, radiofrequency, plasma and other technologies may produce thermal, mechanical, fractional or luminous effects.

49. Does PEIM treat vascular disease?

No. The described PEIM is aesthetic for selected small superficial vessels and does not replace vascular consultation, Doppler or medical treatment.

50. How should complaints and conflicts begin?

The document recommends first using the official clinic or professional channel for clarification, recording, assessment and attempted resolution.
