BENEATH THE SURFACE

What you see is only the beginning

Epidermis

The outer region supports barrier function, pigment production and continual surface renewal.

Dermis

Contains collagen, elastin, vessels, nerve endings, follicles and glands contributing to strength and support.

Supporting tissue

Contributes to cushioning, volume, contour and the relationship between skin and deeper structures.

Why depth matters

A cleanser, peel, CO₂ laser, radiofrequency and focused ultrasound do not act in the same tissue plane.

Concern, target and depth

These three elements should agree before a professional treatment is chosen.

THE SKIN BARRIER

Protect before you intensify

When skin asks for less, I listen.

Signs it may be overwhelmed

  • Tightness after cleansing
  • Persistent dryness or roughness
  • Burning or stinging
  • Increased redness
  • Flaking
  • Sudden reaction to familiar products

Common sources of pressure

  • Too many active ingredients
  • Repeated exfoliation
  • Harsh cleansing
  • Unprotected sun or heat
  • Poorly timed procedures
  • Constantly changing routines

Sometimes the first treatment is repair

Fewer products, gentler cleansing, barrier-supportive moisturising, sun protection and time can create a better foundation.

When the skin is asking for less, adding more is not ambition. It is noise.

03

A USEFUL ROUTINE

Purpose before product count

Morning

  • Cleanse according to need
  • Use a targeted product when appropriate
  • Moisturise for comfort and support
  • Apply broad-spectrum sun protection

Evening

  • Remove makeup and sunscreen
  • Cleanse without scrubbing
  • Use actives at a planned frequency
  • Support the barrier as needed

Before adding another product, ask:

  • What is it intended to do?
  • Am I already using something for the same purpose?
  • Could it irritate another active?
  • How often should I begin?
  • What result would be realistic?
  • Will I use it consistently enough to evaluate it?

A routine is successful when it is clear enough to follow and gentle enough to continue.

05

SKIN MYTHS

Good information protects the skin

“Oily skin does not need moisturiser.”

Oil and water balance are not the same. Oily or acne-prone skin can still become irritated and uncomfortable.

“Blackheads are dirt.”

Blackheads are open comedones. Their dark appearance comes from follicular contents being exposed to air—not poor hygiene.

“Scrubbing clears acne faster.”

Aggressive scrubbing can increase irritation, colour change and discomfort.

“Pores can be permanently closed.”

Pores are normal anatomical openings. Their appearance may be refined, not removed.

“Tingling proves a product is working.”

Tingling can also signal irritation. Effective care does not need to burn.

“More peeling means a better treatment.”

Visible peeling is not a reliable measure of quality and can simply indicate more inflammation.

Skin care becomes more effective when discomfort is no longer mistaken for progress.

06

PIGMENT, SUN AND MEMORY

Colour often has a history

Pigment can appear or deepen after sunlight, acne, irritation, injury, hormonal change, friction, medication or poorly timed procedures.

Pattern

Is it isolated, scattered, symmetrical or widespread?

Trigger

Did it follow acne, pregnancy, irritation, treatment or sun exposure?

Behaviour

Is it fading, stable, deepening or returning?

Skin response

Does the skin inflame or mark easily?

Maintenance

Can the necessary routine and protection be followed?

Medical review

A new, changing, bleeding or unexplained lesion should not be treated as a routine mark.

☼ Sun protection is a system

Broad-spectrum sunscreen, shade, clothing, sensible exposure and reapplication work together. Tinted protection may be discussed for selected pigmentary concerns.

Treating pigment without addressing its triggers is like lowering the volume without turning off the source.

07

ACNE WITHOUT PUNISHMENT

Clearer skin through consistency—not aggression

Acne is a complex inflammatory condition involving the follicle, oil production, cell build-up and microbial activity. It is not evidence that the skin is dirty.

Acne-prone skin often needs

  • Gentle consistent cleansing
  • Gradual introduction of actives
  • Moisture support
  • Sun protection
  • Time to evaluate response
  • Medical assessment when needed

What often makes it harder

  • Picking or squeezing
  • Changing routines repeatedly
  • Several strong ingredients at once
  • Scrubbing inflamed skin
  • Trying to dry every area
  • Treating marks while inflammation continues

The aim is not to make the skin feel attacked enough to change. It is to create a plan it can tolerate long enough to respond.
