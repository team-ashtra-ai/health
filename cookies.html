02

What cookies are

Small files and related storage technologies

Cookies are small text files that a website or service may place in your browser. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, pixels and identifiers used by embedded services.

Some technologies last only for the current browsing session. Others remain until they expire, you delete them, or you change the relevant setting.

This notice uses the word “cookies” as a practical collective term for these technologies unless a distinction is necessary.

05

Retention and details

How long information may remain

  • Session technologies normally expire when the browser session ends.
  • Persistent technologies remain until their stated expiry, until the relevant configuration changes, or until you delete them.
  • A record of your cookie preference may remain until you change the choice, clear browser storage or the website renews consent.
  • Third-party retention periods are controlled by the relevant provider and may vary according to its service and settings.

Keeping this notice accurate

Cookie names, providers and expiry periods can change when a website service is added, removed or reconfigured. The current browser storage panel provides the most direct technical view of active cookies and expiry dates. This notice should be reviewed whenever the website integrations change.
