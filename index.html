The consultation journey

A clear path from concern to considered plan

  1. 01

    We begin with you

    Tell me what you notice, what has changed and what you hope to understand—not what you think you must book.

  2. 02

    I assess the whole picture

    Your history, skin or treatment area, routine, timing and previous experiences are considered together.

  3. 03

    We compare realistic options

    I explain benefits, limitations, preparation, recovery and alternatives in language that is easy to follow.

  4. 04

    You choose the right pace

    The next step may be treatment, preparation, a staged plan, referral, more time—or no treatment at all.

My role is not to persuade you to do more. It is to help you make a decision that feels informed, proportionate and right for you.

Treatment families

Different goals call for different kinds of care

Cleanse and renew

Deep cleansing and carefully selected peels for congestion, texture and surface renewal.

Clarify with laser

Fractional CO₂ laser for appropriate texture, scar and renewal goals with planned recovery.

Ultrasound, radiofrequency and plasma technologies selected according to tissue and treatment goals.

Care for scalp and vessels

Individual strategies for suitable hair-thinning concerns and selected small visible vessels.

Treatment availability and suitability are confirmed during consultation. A treatment shown on the website is never an automatic recommendation for every person.

What shapes a plan

Good planning looks beyond the treatment itself

Your history

Previous treatments, sensitivities, health information and past reactions change what may be appropriate now.

Your everyday life

Work, travel, sun exposure, important events and available recovery time all influence timing.

Your expectations

We discuss what may improve, what may require patience and what cannot honestly be promised.

Your continuity

Preparation, home care, follow-up and maintenance are considered before the first treatment begins.

A treatment can be technically possible and still not be the right choice for your skin, your timing or this moment in your life.

Results in context

Visible change should be understood, not simply displayed

Before-and-after photographs can help show the type and degree of change one person experienced. They cannot predict exactly how another person's skin, hair or tissue will respond.

Real conditions

Lighting, angle, timing and treatment stage should be considered when viewing progress photographs.

Realistic timelines

Some changes appear gradually and may depend on healing, multiple sessions or continued home care.

Individual response

Biology, history, lifestyle and consistency influence the pace, extent and durability of results.

08

Common questions

A few things you may want to know before booking

Do I need to know which treatment to book?

No. Begin with the concern, change or question you would like to discuss. The consultation is used to assess suitability and explain the most appropriate options.

Can treatment happen during the first appointment?

Sometimes, but never automatically. It depends on the procedure, your health history, preparation requirements, available time and whether it is appropriate to proceed that day.

How many sessions will I need?

The number and spacing of sessions vary according to the concern, treatment response, chosen technology, home care and the result that can realistically be achieved.

What happens when a treatment is not suitable?

Franciele may recommend preparation, a different approach, more time, or evaluation by another qualified professional. A responsible plan may also mean deciding not to treat.
