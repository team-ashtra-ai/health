Values in Practice

The principles you can expect to experience in Franciele’s care.

ConsultContact
01

SUITABILITY

Listening

Franciele first asks what you have noticed, what you hope may change and how the concern affects you. You do not need the correct treatment name or clinical language.

You have time

Sensitive concerns can be explained in your own way, with room for questions and uncertainty.

Context is useful

Previous care, routine, health information and practical commitments help shape the advice.

Priorities matter

The result you want and the amount of change you feel comfortable with are discussed openly.

02

CLARITY

Suitability

A procedure is considered because it may fit your concern and circumstances, not because it is new, popular or available.

Individual assessment

Skin characteristics, treatment history, health context and contraindications may affect the choice.

Right timing

Treatment may be delayed when the area needs preparation or recovery would be difficult to manage.

Another route

Franciele may suggest a different approach, observation or assessment by another professional.

03

RESTRAINT

Clarity

You should understand what a recommendation is intended to address and what it cannot promise.

Before deciding

Franciele explains the purpose, alternatives, important limitations and likely treatment sequence.

Before treatment

Preparation, expected temporary reactions and practical restrictions are discussed.

After treatment

You receive relevant aftercare and know when a change should be reported.

04

INDIVIDUALITY

Restraint

More treatment is not automatically better treatment. The plan should have a reason for each step.

Enough can be enough

Franciele may recommend a smaller intervention, fewer treatment areas or time to review.

Sequence matters

Combining procedures only makes sense when their timing and cumulative recovery are appropriate.

Natural movement matters

Facial planning considers expression and recognisable features, not a standardised ideal.

05

SAFETY

Individuality

Your features, skin, routine and preferences are not interchangeable with anyone else’s.

Your starting point

Assessment is based on what is present today, rather than an edited image or another patient’s result.

Your idea of change

Subtle, gradual or more visible goals require different conversations and may have different limits.

Your life

Work, travel, events, sun exposure and available recovery time can affect scheduling.

06

HONESTY

Safety

Safety includes knowing when to adjust, postpone or advise against an elective procedure.

  1. 01

    Screen

    Review relevant health information, medication, sensitivities, recent procedures and current skin condition.

  2. 02

    Prepare

    Give procedure-specific instructions and allow time for the area to become ready when needed.

  3. 03

    Treat

    Use the selected technology or technique for a defined purpose within professional scope.

  4. 04

    Support

    Explain expected recovery, warning signs and the appropriate contact route.

07

RESPECT

Honesty

Results, comfort, recovery and treatment duration differ between people. Franciele discusses that uncertainty directly.

No guaranteed result

Before-and-after images and testimonials are examples, not promises of an identical response.

Limits are part of consent

You should know where improvement may be modest, gradual, temporary or require maintenance.

Plans can change

Your actual response may support continuing, adjusting, simplifying or stopping.

08

CONTINUITY

Respect

Respect means your comfort, privacy and boundaries remain part of the clinical decision.

  • Sensitive concerns are discussed discreetly.
  • Questions are answered without making you feel difficult.
  • You can ask for time, decline an option or change your mind.
  • Clinical photography and any wider use require the appropriate permission.
  • A consultation does not oblige you to have a procedure.
