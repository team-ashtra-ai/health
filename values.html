Core principles

Values become practical in consultation

These principles are not slogans. They shape what is asked, explained, delayed, or recommended.

Listen before suggesting

The first task is understanding the person, not naming a procedure.

Explain clearly

You should know why an option is being discussed and what it requires.

Protect expectations

Responsible language names variation, limits, maintenance, and aftercare.

Respect comfort

Questions about sensitivity, boundaries, and pace belong in the plan.

Plan individually

Routine, timing, history, and goals shape the route.

Use restraint

Sometimes the best guidance is to wait, prepare, or avoid a choice.
