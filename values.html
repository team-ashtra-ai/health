01

SUITABILITY

Available does not always mean appropriate

A device may be advanced and a treatment may be popular. Neither fact confirms that it belongs in your plan.

Concern

What are we actually trying to improve?

Person

What individual factors affect the plan?

Moment

Is the skin ready and is the timing responsible?

Recovery

Can the necessary aftercare be followed?

Expectation

Can the treatment realistically support the goal?

Alternative

Would preparation, referral or waiting be better?

Example

A resurfacing treatment may be technically capable of improving texture, but recent sun exposure or insufficient recovery time may make it unsuitable now.

The right treatment is not simply the treatment that can be performed. It is the treatment that should be performed.

02

CLARITY

Understand before agreeing

I explain

  • Why the option is considered
  • What it is intended to target
  • What it may improve
  • What sensations and recovery may occur
  • What alternatives exist

I also explain

  • What it cannot promise
  • Which risks matter
  • What preparation is required
  • Whether a series may be needed
  • When another evaluation comes first

Example

Pigmentation care should explain not only how colour may improve, but also why marks recur and how protection and continuity influence the result.

Trust grows when the explanation is as careful as the procedure.

03

RESTRAINT

More treatment is not always better treatment

I choose

  • The simplest suitable route
  • Gradual planning
  • Time between treatments
  • Observation before adding more
  • A decision to wait when needed

I avoid

  • Procedures without a clear reason
  • Treating every feature as a flaw
  • Combinations used only to look complete
  • Artificial urgency
  • Intensity for its own sake

Example

When several treatments are requested together, I may recommend a staged sequence so the response can be understood before more is added.

Professional confidence is not shown by doing the most. It is shown by knowing what is enough.

04

INDIVIDUALITY

Keep what makes you recognisable

There is no single ideal face, expression, skin texture or proportion that every person should pursue.

Your features

Not a template copied from someone else.

Your preferences

Subtle and visible change are different requests.

Your expression

Movement and personality belong in facial planning.

Your history

Past procedures and experiences shape what feels right.

Your identity

Care should complement—not standardise.

Your decision

The desired degree of change should be discussed first.

Example

Two people may ask about the same expression line. One wants gentle softening; another may prefer a more visible change. The conversation should reflect that difference.

The most refined result is often the one that still feels unmistakably yours.

05

SAFETY

Safety is built into every decision

  1. 01

    Assess

    Review history, medication, allergies and previous reactions.

  2. 02

    Prepare

    Adjust products, sun exposure or timing when necessary.

  3. 03

    Select

    Choose treatment, product, device and settings for the indication.

  4. 04

    Explain

    Discuss expected effects, risks and alternatives.

  5. 05

    Follow up

    Know what is expected and when to contact the clinic.

Example

A laser may be suitable for a concern, but recent tanning can change risk. Waiting becomes part of safety.

Advanced technology does not replace careful judgment.

06

HONESTY

Honesty before promises

Individual response varies. Some concerns improve without disappearing completely. Some treatments develop gradually or require maintenance.

I discuss honestly

  • Modest possible change
  • The possibility of several sessions
  • Visible recovery
  • Recurrence or maintenance
  • When another route is better

I do not promise

  • Perfect skin
  • An identical result
  • Permanent change in every case
  • A fixed response timeline
  • No risk or no maintenance

Example

Laser hair reduction may significantly reduce suitable hair, but it should not be sold as a guarantee that every hair will disappear forever.

A beautiful promise is not more valuable than an honest explanation.

07

RESPECT

Your comfort and boundaries belong inside the plan

  • Ask before examining or touching a treatment area;
  • Explain what will happen before it happens;
  • Protect privacy;
  • Take discomfort seriously;
  • Avoid judgment about appearance;
  • Respect the decision to pause or decline;
  • Never use insecurity as a sales tool.

In practice

You may ask for another explanation, choose a subtler approach, decide you need more time or say that you do not want to proceed. Consent is not assumed.

Care should never make you feel that you have lost control of the decision.

08

CONTINUITY

Care continues after appointments

  1. 01

    Prepare

    Create the right conditions before treatment.

  2. 02

    Treat

    Use an individually selected approach.

  3. 03

    Protect

    Follow procedure-specific guidance.

  4. 04

    Observe

    Allow the response to develop.

  5. 05

    Review

    Adapt the next step to the actual response.

Example

After a peel, laser or injectable, the next decision should consider how your skin or tissue actually responded—not only the original schedule.

The plan should follow your response—not force your response to follow the plan.
