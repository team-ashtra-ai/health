01

CHOOSING TREATMENT

Suitability before popularity

How does Franciele decide which treatment to recommend?

The recommendation considers your concern, skin or treatment area, health context, previous procedures, routine, timing, comfort, expectations and the recovery required.

Is the newest or strongest treatment always better?

No. A more intense procedure can also involve more inflammation, recovery and risk. The appropriate option is the one that fits the target and individual context.

Can several treatments be combined?

Sometimes, but combination is not automatically better. Treatment sequence, tissue response, cumulative irritation and recovery need to be considered before procedures are combined.

What happens when treatment is not appropriate?

Franciele may recommend preparation, a gentler first step, observation, postponement or another professional evaluation. Responsible consultation does not need to end with a procedure.

02

SKIN AND ROUTINES

Caring without overloading

How many skincare products do I need?

There is no ideal number. A useful routine normally has a clear purpose, suitable cleansing, moisturising support, sun protection and targeted ingredients only when the skin can tolerate them.

Why does my skin sting after products that used to feel fine?

The barrier may be irritated or the routine may have become too intense. Stinging can also have other causes, so persistent or worsening sensitivity deserves assessment.

Does oily skin need moisturiser?

It may. Oil production and water balance are different. A suitable moisturiser can support comfort and tolerance, especially when acne or exfoliating products are used.

Can pores be closed permanently?

No. Pores are normal anatomical structures. Their appearance may be influenced by congestion, oil, texture, elasticity and light, and may be refined rather than removed.

03

ACNE, PIGMENTATION AND SENSITIVITY

Different concerns need different plans

Is acne caused by unclean skin?

No. Acne is a complex inflammatory condition involving the follicle, oil production, cell build-up and microbial activity. Aggressive cleansing and scrubbing can increase irritation.

Are post-acne marks the same as scars?

No. Flat red, brown or grey colour changes are different from structural scars. A person can have marks, scars or both, and each may require a different plan.

Can melasma be permanently removed?

Melasma is commonly persistent and recurrent. Treatment may improve control and appearance, but maintenance and protection from triggers remain important. Permanent disappearance should not be promised.

When should redness or sensitivity be evaluated medically?

Persistent burning, recurrent inflammation, a widespread rash, rapidly worsening redness or suspected infection may require dermatological or medical assessment before elective aesthetic care.

04

LASER FUNDAMENTALS

Understanding the technology

Is IPL a laser?

No. IPL uses a broad spectrum of filtered light. Laser systems use more specific wavelength characteristics. Both may have appropriate uses, but they are not the same technology.

Is laser suitable for every skin tone?

Selected treatments may be suitable across a wide range of skin tones, but not every device, wavelength or setting is appropriate for every skin or target.

Does laser make the skin thinner?

Different lasers have different effects. Hair-reduction and pigment-targeting systems do not work like ablative resurfacing. CO₂ laser intentionally treats microscopic tissue areas to create a controlled healing response.

Is the strongest laser setting the most effective?

No. The correct setting is the one intended to create a useful response while respecting surrounding skin and individual risk.

05

LASER HAIR REDUCTION

Progressive reduction, not instant permanence

Why are several sessions normally needed?

Hair grows in cycles, and only a proportion is in a responsive phase at each appointment. A series allows treatment at different growth stages.

Does laser remove every hair permanently?

The more accurate description is long-term hair reduction. Suitable hair may become less dense, finer or slower to regrow, while some remaining or newly developing hair may require maintenance.

Which hair responds less well?

Very light, grey, white and some red hair may respond poorly because it contains less of the pigment used as a target by many hair-reduction lasers.

Can I arrive recently tanned?

Recent tanning can change energy absorption and may increase risk. Sun exposure should be disclosed so the appointment can be assessed or postponed when needed.

06

PEELS AND CO₂ RESURFACING

Depth changes recovery

Does more visible peeling mean a better chemical peel?

No. Visible peeling is not a reliable measure of treatment quality. Peel depth, inflammation and recovery should match the indication rather than pursue the most dramatic reaction.

Is TCA always a deep peel?

No. TCA depth depends on concentration, formulation, skin preparation, application technique and individual response.

Is fractional CO₂ a superficial treatment?

No. Fractional treatment leaves untreated tissue between microscopic treatment zones, but it still intentionally disrupts the barrier and can involve meaningful recovery.

Can laser completely erase acne scars?

Laser may soften selected scar characteristics, but it cannot restore injured skin exactly to its previous state. Scar type often determines whether a combination approach is needed.

07

INJECTABLES AND FIRMNESS

Realistic, anatomy-led planning

Does botulinum toxin remove expression forever?

No. It temporarily reduces activity in selected muscles. The effect develops and later diminishes, and treatment should respect anatomy, expression and individual preference.

Is the Nefertiti technique the same as a surgical neck lift?

No. Botulinum toxin may soften selected platysma bands or muscular pull, but it does not reposition tissue like surgery.

Can Ultraformer or radiofrequency replace surgery?

No. Selected energy-based treatments may support mild-to-moderate tightening or contour goals, but they do not produce the same changes as surgery.

Do hair mesotherapy or MMP guarantee hair growth?

No. Evidence and suitability depend on the cause of hair loss, the substance used and the treatment protocol. Hair loss may also require medical investigation.

08

PREPARATION AND RECOVERY

Aftercare belongs inside the plan

Should I stop medication before treatment?

Never stop prescribed medication on your own. Disclose what you take, and any necessary change should be discussed with the professional responsible for prescribing it.

When can I restart acids or retinoids?

The timing depends on the procedure and the condition of the skin barrier. Follow the individual instructions provided rather than a generic number of days.

Can I exercise after treatment?

Some procedures require a temporary change in exercise, heat or pressure. The recommendation depends on what was performed and should be explained specifically.

When should I contact the clinic after treatment?

Contact the clinic when recovery differs from what was explained, especially for increasing pain, spreading redness or heat, blistering, discharge, a wound, marked colour change or persistent altered sensation.
