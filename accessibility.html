01

OUR INTENTION

What We Aim For

  • Logical heading order and page structure;
  • Readable text with sufficient contrast;
  • Keyboard navigation for interactive elements;
  • Visible focus states for links, buttons and form fields;
  • Alternative text for meaningful images;
  • Labels and instructions for forms;
  • Content that remains understandable when enlarged;
  • Reduced reliance on colour alone to communicate meaning.

Continuous improvement

The website may evolve as pages, tools and technologies change. Accessibility checks should continue whenever new content or components are introduced.

02

READING AND NAVIGATION

Read and Navigate Your Way

Zoom

Use your browser controls to enlarge text and page content.

Keyboard

Move through links and form fields using Tab and Shift + Tab.

Screen readers

Headings and labels are intended to support assistive navigation.

Language

Use the English or Portuguese version according to your preference.

Motion

Animations should respect reduced-motion preferences where supported.

Contact alternatives

WhatsApp and email remain available when a form is difficult to use.

03

FORMS

Forms with Clear Guidance

Contact and consultation forms should identify every field, explain why information is requested and communicate errors in text—not only through colour.

  • Required fields are marked clearly;
  • Instructions appear near the relevant field;
  • Error messages explain what needs to be corrected;
  • Success messages confirm when a submission has been received;
  • Time limits should not prevent a person from completing a form.
04

IMAGES AND MEDIA

Images with Useful Meaning

Meaningful clinical and editorial images should include concise alternative text. Decorative imagery may be hidden from assistive technology when it does not add information.

Videos should be supported by captions or transcripts whenever video content is published. Important instructions should also appear in text.

05

CONTENT CLARITY

Plain, Professional Language

Aesthetic and skin-care terminology can be unfamiliar. I aim to explain important concepts in direct language while preserving clinical accuracy.

Helpful content

  • Short paragraphs and descriptive headings
  • Treatment purpose and limitations explained together
  • Clear routes into related pages
  • Plain-language form guidance

What we try to avoid

  • Instructions hidden in images
  • Links labelled only “click here”
  • Dense blocks without structure
  • Colour as the only signal
06

KNOWN LIMITATIONS

Where Barriers May Remain

Some third-party services, embedded tools or external platforms may not offer the same level of accessibility as the main website. Their behaviour can also change independently.

If a feature prevents you from accessing information or contacting the clinic, please use another contact route and tell us what happened.
