01

MY CARE PHILOSOPHY

Your concern deserves more than a treatment menu

You do not need to arrive knowing the name of a treatment. You may come with a change you have noticed, a previous experience you want to discuss or a result you would like to understand.

I begin with that context rather than immediately directing you towards a procedure.

  • Context before procedure names;
  • Assessment before recommendation;
  • Natural-looking outcomes without standardising every person;
  • Honest explanations without guaranteed results;
  • Preparation and aftercare as part of the treatment;
  • Space to ask, reconsider or wait.

The right plan should make sense for your skin, routine, timing and comfort—not only for the concern you can see.

HOW I BUILD A PLAN

A decision made in stages

  1. 01

    Listen

    Understand what brought you here and what matters to you.

  2. 02

    Review

    Discuss history, routine, sensitivities, health context and timing.

  3. 03

    Assess

    Evaluate the skin, scalp, face, body area or vascular concern.

  4. 04

    Explain

    Clarify options, limits, sensations, risks and recovery.

  5. 05

    Decide

    Treat, prepare, stage, refer, observe or wait.

Evaluation first. Then, the appropriate next step.

A consultation may lead to a procedure, but its first purpose is to create clarity.

03

BEFORE TREATMENT

What I need to understand before we proceed

Health and medication

Relevant conditions, allergies, medication, supplements, pregnancy, breastfeeding and recent illness may affect planning.

Previous treatments

Lasers, peels, injectables, surgery, threads, tattoos and reactions can remain relevant.

Current products

Retinoids, acids, acne products, depigmenting formulas and home devices may affect tolerance.

Sun and heat exposure

Recent tanning, sunburn or planned outdoor activity can change treatment timing.

Your calendar

Travel, work, exercise, celebrations and photographs help determine an appropriate recovery window.

Your expectations

We discuss what may improve, what may require several stages and what cannot be promised.

The treatment date should suit the recovery—not force the recovery to suit the date.

04

DIFFERENT TREATMENTS, DIFFERENT RECOVERY

Aftercare must reflect what was actually performed

Cleansing and gentle renewal

Deep cleansing, diamond peel, crystal peel and ultrasonic peel may require a simple, calming routine and temporary pause from irritating products.

Chemical renewal

Retinoic, Jessner, TCA and depigmentation protocols vary in depth. Flaking, redness and product restrictions depend on the exact peel.

Laser and resurfacing

Hair reduction, pigment treatment and ablative CO₂ resurfacing have very different recovery needs.

Firmness and energy-based care

Focused ultrasound, radiofrequency and plasma technologies differ in target, depth, sensation and aftercare.

Injectables and microinfusion

Botulinum toxin, MMP and scalp injections require treatment-specific guidance on pressure, products and activity.

Superficial microvessels

PEIM aftercare depends on the vessels treated, sclerosant used and individual vascular assessment.

05

AFTERCARE FOUNDATIONS

Protect the treated area

The instructions I provide after your appointment take priority over general website information.

  • Use only the cleansers, moisturisers and repair products recommended for the procedure;
  • Avoid repeated touching, rubbing, scratching, squeezing or pressing;
  • Allow flakes, peeling or small crusts to release naturally;
  • Follow the sun-protection plan discussed for your treatment;
  • Ask before restarting retinoids, acids, scrubs or depigmenting products;
  • Keep anything that contacts the treated area clean.

A stronger product does not necessarily create a faster or better result.

06

DOS AND DON’TS

Small decisions can protect the work already done

Do

  • Follow your individual instructions
  • Keep the routine simple and consistent
  • Respect the recovery window
  • Send clear photographs when requested
  • Ask before combining treatments
  • Contact me when uncertain

Avoid

  • Picking, scratching or peeling
  • Copying another patient’s routine
  • Adding strong active products early
  • Unprotected sun exposure
  • Judging every result immediately
  • Treating aftercare as optional

Aftercare is not an extra step. It is part of responsible treatment.

07

DAILY LIFE DURING RECOVERY

What may need to change temporarily

Exercise

Intense activity may need to be postponed after selected injectables, vascular care or deeper resurfacing.

Heat

Hot showers, saunas, steam and prolonged heat may be unsuitable during early recovery.

Swimming

Pools, hot tubs, lakes and the sea may need to wait while the barrier is disrupted.

Makeup

It may be allowed quickly after some procedures and restricted after others.

Shaving and waxing

Hair-removal methods may need to be adjusted around laser, peels or resurfacing.

Massage and pressure

Do not massage injectable or energy-treated areas unless specifically instructed.

Individual guidance only

I do not impose every possible restriction after every treatment. I explain what applies to the procedure you actually received.

08

CONTACT AND SAFETY

Questions are part of follow-up care

Temporary redness, warmth, tenderness, swelling, bruising, dryness or peeling may occur after some procedures. The expected response depends on the treatment and should be explained before you leave.

  • Contact me if pain is severe or increasing;
  • Contact me if redness, swelling or heat continues to spread;
  • Contact me for blistering, discharge, an unexpected wound or marked colour change;
  • Contact me if altered sensation persists or intensifies;
  • Contact me before resuming a product or activity when you are unsure.

Seek urgent medical care

Do not wait for a routine clinic reply for difficulty breathing, speaking or swallowing; rapid facial or throat swelling; fainting; severe generalised weakness; significant visual disturbance; chest pain; sudden shortness of breath; coughing blood; or another serious, rapidly developing reaction.
