Assessment

Laser is not a one-size-fits-all decision

Franciele considers indication, skin response, recent exposure, sensitivity, timing, preparation, and aftercare before discussing laser as a route.

  1. Check the context. Skin condition, recent sun exposure, sensitivity, and prior treatments matter.
  2. Discuss preparation. Some choices may need waiting, barrier support, or routine adjustments first.
  3. Plan aftercare. Comfort, recovery, and protection should be understood before moving forward.

Suitability checklist

Laser planning should name the variables

The goal is not to make laser sound dramatic. It is to understand whether the timing, skin condition, and aftercare responsibilities are appropriate.

Exposure and timing

Recent sun exposure, travel, outdoor plans, events, and recovery windows can change when laser makes sense.

Sensation and downtime

Questions about comfort, redness, and aftercare should be discussed before treatment.

Expected response

Results depend on indication, sessions, consistency, and individual skin response.

Preparation and aftercare

A laser plan includes what happens around the appointment

The responsible conversation includes what to avoid before, how to protect the skin after, and what signs or sensations should be expected or reported.

Questions worth asking

  • Before: what routine, exposure, or skin condition could make waiting wiser?
  • During: what sensation is expected, and how will comfort be handled?
  • After: what protection, recovery care, and follow-up timing are needed?
